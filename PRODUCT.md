# Product

## Register

product

## Users

QuoteApp is used by freelancers and small business owners who need to manage commercial work quickly: prepare quotes, convert accepted quotes into invoices, track payments, manage clients, organize projects, record expenses, and understand business performance. Clients also interact with public quote, invoice, and portal views to review documents, approve work, and submit payment proof.

## Product Purpose

QuoteApp exists to make the quote-to-cash workflow clear, professional, and efficient for small operators who do not want enterprise complexity. Success means users can understand business status at a glance, create polished documents quickly, follow up on unpaid work confidently, and give clients a trustworthy document experience.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, professional. The product should feel dependable and premium without becoming decorative or slow. It should communicate financial confidence, operational clarity, and practical momentum.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards that rely on identical card grids, decorative gradients, glassmorphism, oversized empty hero sections, weak gray text, and vague "modern" styling. Avoid interfaces that look like a landing page instead of a working business tool. Avoid cluttered navigation, ambiguous payment status, cramped tables, and mobile layouts that merely shrink desktop screens.

## Design Principles

1. Make status obvious before asking users to act.
2. Keep repeated workflows fast: quote, invoice, client, item, expense, and payment actions should be near the work.
3. Treat public documents as trust surfaces; they must look polished and be easy for clients to understand.
4. Use visual hierarchy to support scanning, comparison, and prioritization.
5. Preserve operational density without sacrificing readability or touch comfort.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible interaction patterns. Do not rely on color alone for status. Support reduced motion, clear focus states, readable financial numbers, accessible labels for icon buttons, and mobile layouts without horizontal overflow.
